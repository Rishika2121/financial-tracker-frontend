Presumptive Taxation in India

Under presumptive taxation, the government allows eligible taxpayers to declare income at fixed prescribed rates without maintaining detailed books of accounts.

Section 44AD – Small Businesses

Eligible For:

  • Resident Individual / HUF
  • Partnership Firm (Non-LLP)

Turnover Limits:

  • ₹2 Crore – Normal
  • ₹3 Crore – If cash receipts & payments ≤ 5%

Presumptive Income:

  • 8% – Normal receipts
  • 6% – Digital receipts

If lower income declared: Books + Audit required

Section 44ADA – Professionals

Covered Professions:

  • CA, CS, CMA
  • Doctor, Engineer, Architect
  • Lawyer, Consultant, IT Professional

Receipt Limits:

  • ₹50 Lakh – Normal
  • ₹75 Lakh – If cash ≤ 5%

Presumptive Income:

  • 50% of gross receipts

Section 44AE – Transport Business

Eligible For:

  • Individual, HUF, Firm, Company, LLP
  • Only Goods Carriages (Max 10 vehicles)

Presumptive Income:

  • Heavy Vehicle (≥12 ton) – ₹1,000 per ton per month
  • Other Vehicle – ₹7,500 per month per vehicle

Example:

2 Trucks (12 ton) × ₹1,000 × 12 months = ₹2,88,000 presumptive income.
