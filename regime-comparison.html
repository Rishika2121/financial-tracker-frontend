Old vs New Tax Regime Comparison Engine

Provision-based comparison using government-notified slabs, rebates, surcharge, cess, and marginal relief rules.

Old Tax Regime

Income Tax Slabs

  • ₹0 – ₹2,50,000 → Nil
  • ₹2,50,001 – ₹5,00,000 → 5%
  • ₹5,00,001 – ₹10,00,000 → 20%
  • Above ₹10,00,000 → 30%

Basic Exemption Limit

  • Senior Citizen (60–80): ₹3,00,000
  • Super Senior Citizen (80+): ₹5,00,000

Rebate – Section 87A

  • Total income ≤ ₹5,00,000
  • Rebate up to ₹12,500
  • Effective tax becomes NIL

Surcharge

  • ₹50L–₹1Cr → 10%
  • ₹1Cr–₹2Cr → 15%
  • ₹2Cr–₹5Cr → 25%
  • Above ₹5Cr → 37%

Cess

  • 4% Health & Education Cess

New Tax Regime

Income Tax Slabs (Before Cess)

  • Up to ₹4,00,000 → 0% (No tax)
  • ₹4,00,001 – ₹8,00,000 → 5%
  • ₹8,00,001 – ₹12,00,000 → 10%
  • ₹12,00,001 – ₹16,00,000 → 15%
  • ₹16,00,001 – ₹20,00,000 → 20%
  • ₹20,00,001 – ₹24,00,000 → 25%
  • Above ₹24,00,000 → 30%

Rebate – Section 87A (New Regime)

  • Applicable if total income ≤ ₹7,00,000
  • Rebate up to ₹25,000
  • Effective tax becomes NIL

Standard Deduction

  • ₹50,000 allowed for salaried & pensioners

Surcharge

  • ₹50L–₹1Cr → 10%
  • ₹1Cr–₹2Cr → 15%
  • ₹2Cr–₹5Cr → 25%
  • Above ₹5Cr → Maximum 25%

Cess

  • 4% Health & Education Cess on computed tax

Marginal Relief (Both Regimes)

Marginal relief ensures that additional tax payable due to surcharge does not exceed the actual income increase beyond the surcharge limit.

Example:
Income = ₹50,10,000
Extra income = ₹10,000
Tax increase without relief = ₹1,20,000
Tax payable restricted to ₹10,000
